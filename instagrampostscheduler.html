The Problem

The is no current way to natively schedule Instagram posts ahead of time. The current solutions are platforms to crate posts and a reminder at the designated publishing time. These solutions are basically alarm clocks because they lack the abilty to automatically post at a designated time.

My Role

Unique Value Proposition

The addition of a native post scheduler on Instagram's mobile application will give Instagram users the ability to set and forget their posts and add aditional value to Instagram's business

Research

To start our process, we conducted competitive research, user interviews, and created proto-personas

Competitive Research

We identified multiple possible solutions, affirming that there is a need for this; however, are the current solutions sufficient?

Interviews and Proto-Personas

By conducting interviews, we identified 2 proto personas

First Problem

We realized that Karen Swanson, a social media manager, plans all of her social media on the desktop and was not suitable for a mobile app solution. This is because a social media manager working on dozens of accounts needs the screen real estate that a desktop provides.

Thus, we created a follow up persona and redefined the problem.

Redefined Problem + Personas

There is no current solution to schedule Instagram posts for personal account power users (fitness models, popular pet accounts, average user who posts frequently)

Storyboarding and User Journey Mapping

We then created a storyboard and user journey map to show how our personas would benefit from using our proposed solution

Storyboard

Paper Protyping and User Testing

We wanted to insert this feature without disrupting the flow of the normal average user, someone who doesn't need to schedule posts. We kept that in mind when creating the paper prototypes

User Flow Update

After getting feedback for our user flow, we identified parts of the flow that were superfluous to our persona’s user goal, like notifications. We removed the notifications and concentrated on a more streamlined user flow.

Challenges & Future Plans

One of our key findings during our process was identifying one of our initial personas was not appropriate for our mobile solution. T his was due to the constant task that social media managers do most of their work on desktop because they need more screen real estate.

After we ran into our first problem, we wanted to validate our persona, so we sent out a Facebook survey. That survey simply asked personal account Instagram users if they would use a post scheduler. 40% of users said that they would, which was a lot higher than we expected. We’d like to dig deeper into our personas to figure out what the artists, the Instagram celebrities really need because they are the contributors of Instagram, even though they make up the minority amount of the DAU. It’s these people who give Instagram content and added value.
