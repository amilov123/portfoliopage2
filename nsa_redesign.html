Our Task: Redesign the Current NSA Website

The NSA

The NSA protects the United States through cybersecurity, cryptology, signals intelligence, and computer network operations to give the United States and its allies decision advantage

Evaluation of the Current NSA Website

When we examined the current NSA website we found that the current NSA website is very text heavy with very little use of images; The existing sitemap is very extensive. We felt that the sitemap could be reorganized and condensed. We also thought that the main menu had to many items with subject matter that both overlaps and is also repetitive.

Card Sorting

We performed a card sort to organize categories that make sense to help design our information architecture

Sitemap

We constructed a sitemap based on our card sort

Formulating a Style Guide

We created a style guide

A/B Testing

We designed two possible navigation patterns: tabbed navigation and a hamburger menu. We were curious to see which would be more easily understood by users.

Hamburger Menu

Tabbed Navigation

A/B Testing Data

There was no real winner between the two types of navigation. People were evenly split in preference for each type of navigation.

Conclusions from A/B Testing

Because there was no preference for either navigation pattern we decided to go with tabbed navigation because that is a modern trend

Further Condensing the Sitemap

During the design process we decided the "about us" category could be combined with the "what we do" category producing a much more condensed primary navigation
