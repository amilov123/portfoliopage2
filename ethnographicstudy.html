Ethnographic Research Study of Pinches Tacos

Project Overview

Our group conducted an ethnographic research study of a local Mexican restaurant chain, Pinches Tacos. Our goal was to discover pain points, customer demographics and customer experiences and how Pinches Tacos could increase their business. All customers surveyed liked Pinches Tacos as it is and reported they would make no changes and would not utilize online ordering nor delivery. I found that I could still infer a market that justifies a website redesign that includes using a takeout or delivery service solution such as uber eats or grubhub if pinches can maintain a sufficient profit margin after the costs of using the solution's service are taken into account

My Role

I researched the background of Pinches Tacos and interviewed customers to learn about customer views and craft a user persona illustrating pain points and experiences of customers. I then created a storyboard of an actual visit to Pinches Tacos. I conducted background business research to determine the costs and benefits of utilizing an online takeout or delivery service such as grubhub or ubereats to verify a market for such a website redesign

Initial Hypotheses and Initial Proposed Solutions

During researching the background of Pinches Tacos and studying Pinches Tacos website we hypothesized that rebuilding the Pinches Tacos website and building a functioning online ordering system to replace their nonfunctional order online link was a desirable goal.

The Interviews Contradicted Our Initial Hypotheses; However Market Research Allows Us to Infer a Large Market Justifying a Website Design

After interviewing some Pinches Taco's customers. I determined that although Pinches Taco's website could indeed look better and function better, if my interviewees are indeed representative of the restaurant chains existing customers as a whole I could find no evidence from customer interviews that ordering over the web was a desired service. Every customer I interviewed said that they would not order online and that they prefer to place their orders in person. However, I conducted background Market Research that allows us to infer a large untapped market of those who would utilize online takeout or online delivery ordering. Future Research Goals and Revised Potential Recommendations

To verify whether Pinches Tacos profit margin is sufficient to allow Pinches Tacos to utilize an third party online delivery solution or takeout ordering solution to tap the large market that exists for online delivery and onlie takeout, I would examine how Pinches Tacos profit margin is impacted by the fees that such solutions as Uber Eats and GrubHub charge. If the projections from such analysis are insufficient from a third party online delivery solution, then I would suggest a redesign allowing customers to click to call to place a takeout order. Currently the online ordering button link is non-functional. Pinches Tacos may well be able to increase their business via their website, but constructing an online ordering system of their own may not be indicated if my interviewees are representative. â€‹ Pinches Tacos may be better served by driving more business from their website by a redesign that simply directs the visitors to their website to use such services as Uber Eats and Postmates to order food and removing nonfunctional online ordering links. Using third-parties to handle delivery might better drive new business. Running their own online ordering system could have large labor costs. In addition, being listed as a restaurant choice on delivery apps could enhance Pinches Tacos exposure and introduce the restaurant chain to new customers more efficiently due to the large user base such third-party apps have. To generate pickup orders Pinches Tacos could use a simple click to call link to allow customers to phone in pick-up orders and catering orders or again utilize the strategy of directing those desiring pickup to a contracted third-party app or apps to take their pickup order.
